The Marrow System

01

Tax spending, not wages

A progressive consumption tax replaces most income tax, so the more you buy, the higher your rate, while savings and investment stay untouched. Instead of punishing work, we tax consumption—encouraging saving and investing while making luxury spending fund public goods.

02

Tax land, not buildings

An annual land-value tax captures unearned location gains and is almost impossible to dodge or distort. Property owners pay based on their land's value, not what they build on it—incentivizing development while making land speculation unprofitable.

03

Price pollution and trading churn

Add a carbon fee that is rebated to everyone and a tiny securities-settlement levy. Environmental costs get priced in while excessive financial trading faces friction. The carbon fee goes straight back to citizens as dividends.

04

AI autopilot

An open-source algorithm watches inequality, demographics, and emissions, then nudges the tax bands every quarter; any change is logged on a public Git ledger. No political games—just data-driven adjustments that anyone can verify and audit.

05

Universal give-back

Revenue funds free healthcare, K-14 schooling, childcare, and a sliding cash credit, plus a citizen dividend invested in a national fund. Everyone gets the basics covered, with additional support scaling based on need and a universal dividend for all.

Result: Nordic-level equality with lower payroll taxes and full on-chain transparency.
